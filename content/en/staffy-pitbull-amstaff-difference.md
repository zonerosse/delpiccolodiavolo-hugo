  <div class="lead"><strong>In brief:</strong> Despite superficial similarities, the Staffordshire Bull Terrier, American Pit Bull Terrier and American Staffordshire Terrier are distinct breeds with different histories, sizes and recognition status.</div>
  <h2>Staffordshire Bull Terrier</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Origin:</strong> England (Staffordshire region)</li>
  <li><strong>Size:</strong> 35.5-40.5 cm, 11-17 kg - smallest of the three</li>
  <li><strong>Recognition:</strong> FCI, KC, AKC recognized</li>
  <li><strong>Character:</strong> Affectionate "nanny dog," companion-oriented</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>American Pit Bull Terrier (APBT)</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Origin:</strong> USA</li>
  <li><strong>Size:</strong> 43-53 cm, 14-27 kg</li>
  <li><strong>Recognition:</strong> UKC recognized, NOT AKC</li>
  <li><strong>Character:</strong> Athletic, working dog heritage</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>American Staffordshire Terrier (AmStaff)</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Origin:</strong> USA</li>
  <li><strong>Size:</strong> 43-48 cm, 18-32 kg</li>
  <li><strong>Recognition:</strong> AKC, FCI recognized</li>
  <li><strong>Character:</strong> Show-bred version of APBT</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Why it matters</h2>
  <p>Breed-specific legislation often confuses these breeds. A Staffordshire Bull Terrier is legally distinct and typically exempt from restrictions targeting "pit bull type" dogs in many jurisdictions.</p>
