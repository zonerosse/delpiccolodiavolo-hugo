  <div class="lead">
  <strong>In brief:</strong> The first outdoor walks are crucial for your Staffordshire Bull Terrier puppy's development. Wait for vaccination coverage, choose the right equipment, and focus on positive experiences rather than distance covered.
  </div>
  
  <h2>When to start</h2>
  <ul>
  <li>Wait 7-10 days after second vaccination for immune protection</li>
  <li>Usually around 12-14 weeks of age</li>
  <li>Before that: carry puppy in arms to experience outdoor sounds and sights safely</li>
  <li>Consult your vet for local disease risk assessment</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h2>Essential equipment</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Y-harness:</strong> Better than collar for puppies - distributes pressure across chest</li>
  <li><strong>Fixed leash 1.5-2m:</strong> No retractable leashes - they teach pulling</li>
  <li><strong>Treat pouch:</strong> Quick access to rewards</li>
  <li><strong>High-value treats:</strong> Soft, small, quick to eat</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h2>Introducing harness and leash at home</h2>
  <p>Start indoors before venturing outside:</p>
  <ul>
  <li>Let puppy sniff harness, reward interest</li>
  <li>Put harness on briefly during play, remove before discomfort</li>
  <li>Attach leash and let it drag (supervised) around the house</li>
  <li>Practice following a treat lure with leash attached</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h2>First outdoor sessions</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Duration:</strong> 5-10 minutes maximum - quality over quantity</li>
  <li><strong>Location:</strong> Quiet areas first - avoid crowded, noisy spots</li>
  <li><strong>Focus:</strong> Sniffing and exploring, not distance</li>
  <li><strong>Pace:</strong> Follow puppy's lead, stop when they want to investigate</li>
  </ul>
  
  <div class="callout">
  <p class="callout-title">üêæ Breeder's advice</p>
  <p>Staffordshire Bull Terriers are naturally enthusiastic. Don't rush leash training. A few positive 5-minute sessions build better habits than one frustrating 30-minute walk.</p>
  </div>
  
  <h2>Common mistakes to avoid</h2>
  <ul>
  <li>Walking too far - puppies tire quickly</li>
  <li>Pulling back when puppy pulls - creates opposition reflex</li>
  <li>Forcing through fear - let puppy set the pace for new experiences</li>
  <li>Expecting perfect walking immediately - it takes months of practice</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h2>Building good habits</h2>
  <ul>
  <li>Reward attention and check-ins with you</li>
  <li>Stop and wait when leash goes tight, reward when it loosens</li>
  <li>Make walks about exploration and bonding, not exercise</li>
  <li>End on a positive note before puppy gets overtired</li>
  </ul>
