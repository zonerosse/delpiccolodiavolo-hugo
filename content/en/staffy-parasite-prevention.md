  <div class="lead"><strong>In brief:</strong> Regular parasite prevention protects your Staffordshire Bull Terrier from fleas, ticks and intestinal worms that can cause serious health problems.</div>
  <h2>External parasites</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Fleas:</strong> Monthly preventatives (spot-on, tablets, collars)</li>
  <li><strong>Ticks:</strong> Check after walks, especially in grass/woodland. Use tick preventatives.</li>
  <li><strong>Mites:</strong> Cause mange - vet treatment required</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Internal parasites</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Roundworms:</strong> Deworm puppies every 2-4 weeks until 12 weeks, then monthly until 6 months</li>
  <li><strong>Tapeworms:</strong> Often from fleas - treat with appropriate wormer</li>
  <li><strong>Heartworm:</strong> Monthly prevention essential in endemic areas</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Prevention schedule</h2>
  <p><strong>Monthly:</strong> Flea/tick prevention year-round<br>
  <strong>Quarterly:</strong> Intestinal wormer for adult dogs (more frequently for puppies)<br>
  <strong>Annually:</strong> Fecal test at vet check-up</p>
