  <div class="lead">
  <strong>In brief:</strong> Parasites can cause serious health problems in your Staffordshire Bull Terrier. A regular prevention program against fleas, ticks and intestinal worms is essential for your dog's health and your family's safety.
  </div>
  
  <h2>External Parasites: Fleas and Ticks</h2>
  
  <h3>Fleas</h3>
  
  <p>Fleas are more than just an annoyance - they can cause:</p>
  
  <ul>
  <li>Intense itching and skin irritation</li>
  <li>Flea allergy dermatitis (FAD)</li>
  <li>Tapeworm transmission</li>
  <li>Anemia in severe infestations</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h3>Ticks</h3>
  
  <p>Ticks are dangerous because they transmit serious diseases:</p>
  
  <ul>
  <li>Lyme disease (borreliosis)</li>
  <li>Ehrlichiosis</li>
  <li>Babesiosis</li>
  <li>Anaplasmosis</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h3>Prevention Products</h3>
  
  <p>Options for external parasite prevention include:</p>
  
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Spot-on treatments:</strong> Applied monthly to the skin (e.g., Frontline, Advantix)</li>
  <li><strong>Oral tablets:</strong> Monthly or 3-monthly options (e.g., NexGard, Bravecto)</li>
  <li><strong>Collars:</strong> Long-lasting protection (e.g., Seresto - up to 8 months)</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h2>Internal Parasites: Worms</h2>
  
  <h3>Common Intestinal Worms</h3>
  
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Roundworms:</strong> Very common in puppies</li>
  <li><strong>Tapeworms:</strong> Often transmitted by fleas</li>
  <li><strong>Hookworms:</strong> Can cause anemia</li>
  <li><strong>Whipworms:</strong> Cause diarrhea and weight loss</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h3>Heartworm</h3>
  
  <p>Heartworm (Dirofilaria) is transmitted by mosquitoes and is potentially fatal. Prevention is essential in endemic areas.</p>
  
  <h3>Deworming Schedule</h3>
  
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Puppies:</strong> Every 2 weeks until 12 weeks old, then monthly until 6 months</li>
  <li><strong>Adults:</strong> Every 3-6 months, depending on lifestyle and risk</li>
  <li><strong>Breeding females:</strong> Before mating and during pregnancy as advised by vet</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Creating a Prevention Calendar</h2>
  
  <p>Work with your vet to create a year-round prevention plan based on:</p>
  
  <ul>
  <li>Your geographic location and climate</li>
  <li>Your dog's lifestyle (indoor/outdoor, contact with other animals)</li>
  <li>Local parasite prevalence</li>
  <li>Your Staffy's health status</li>
  </ul>
