  <div class="lead"><strong>In brief:</strong> The breed standard defines the ideal Staffordshire Bull Terrier. Understanding it helps recognize quality specimens and appreciate what responsible breeders work to preserve.</div>
  <h2>General appearance</h2>
  <p>Smooth-coated, well balanced, muscular, active and agile. Great strength for size. Despite muscular appearance, should never appear coarse or lack agility.</p>
  <h2>Size</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Height:</strong> 35.5-40.5 cm (14-16 inches) at withers</li>
  <li><strong>Weight:</strong> Dogs 12.7-17 kg (28-38 lbs), Bitches 11-15.4 kg (24-34 lbs)</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Head</h2>
  <ul>
  <li>Short, deep with broad skull</li>
  <li>Pronounced cheek muscles</li>
  <li>Distinct stop</li>
  <li>Short foreface</li>
  <li>Black nose</li>
  <li>Dark eyes preferred, round</li>
  <li>Rose or half-pricked ears</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Body and movement</h2>
  <ul>
  <li>Level topline, short and muscular</li>
  <li>Deep, wide chest</li>
  <li>Well-sprung ribs</li>
  <li>Free, powerful movement with economy of effort</li>
  <li>Legs moving parallel when viewed from front/rear</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Temperament</h2>
  <p>Traditionally "nanny dog" - affectionate especially with children. Bold, fearless, totally reliable. Highly intelligent, with great strength of character.</p>
